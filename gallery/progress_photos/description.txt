1000037104.jpeg: This photo shows me testing the feather shapes that I've created and printed - sorry, not printed, cut out into cardboard. The feathers are at various stages here. Some of them are just flat, showing how they look immediately after being cut out. Others have been shaped a little bit more, with gentle curves and bends that mimic real hummingbird feathers.<br><br>My primary inspiration comes from feather reference photo libraries where I study how real feathers layer and curve. I'm also deeply inspired by paper artists like <a href="https://www.johanscherft.nl/" target="_blank">Johan Scherft</a> and <a href="https://www.stinebergevensen.nl/" target="_blank">Stine Bergevensen</a>. Their work with paper textures and structural connections has been invaluable to my process.<br><br>This cardboard testing phase is crucial - it allows me to iterate quickly without wasting expensive art paper. Each failed shape teaches me something about where the paper needs to bend, where it needs to stay rigid, and how the feathers should overlap.
